Ingredients:
 - 2 pounds chicken breast
 - 2 heads broccoli
 - 4 medium onions
 - 4 carrots
 - 2 jalapenos
 - 4 cloves garlic
 - 1/2 inch peice ginger
 - 1 green onion
 - peanut oil
 - soy sauce (tamari)
 - crushed red pepper flakes
 - sugar
 - rice wine (Chinese cooking wine)

Directions:
 1. Cut stir-fry ingredients (chicken, broccoli, onions, carrots). Mince garlic and ginger. Slice
 	green onions thinly.
 
 2. In a medium bowl marinade chicken with rice wine and soy sauce.
 
 3. Mix a sauce of 1/2 cup soy sauce. Add crushed red pepper and sugar to taste. Add some of the
 	minced garlic and ginger.

 4. Heat wok with 2 Tbs peanut oil over high heat. Add vegetable ingredients little by little and 
 	stir regularly. When the oil begins to smoke start with the onions. When these are starting to 
 	look cooked add the garlic, ginger, and jalapenos. Next add broccoli. Then add carrots.

 5. Remove cooked vegatables from the wok and set aside in a medium bowl.

 6. Heat another 2 Tbs peanut oil, and stir-fry the chicken until cooked.

 7. Add the vegatables back to the wok with the chicken. Pour the sauce into the wok and bring to a
 	boil. Finally, add the green onions.
